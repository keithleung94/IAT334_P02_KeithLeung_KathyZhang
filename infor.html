Our Brand

The Alleche brand speaks to blooming females looking to upgrade their attractiveness to the next level. Our goal is to provide the best perfume for every single women. ALLECHE has operating over 80 years and is currently the best-selling perfume brand in British. Each year we will be launch a new series, for 2020, it is our FLORAL COLOGNE.

Our Story

Alleche launched her own fragrance brand in 1988. She began combining ingredients at her kitchen table with four plastic jugs and two saucepans. She started with twelve clients who bought her homemade skin creams. A scent she invented called Lime Basil & Mandarin put her on the map. “Fragrance not only flooded me with ideas but it made me feel complete, fueling an almost obsessive drive of creativity,” Alleche writes in her book. “I see smells in colors and memories, and I hear tunes when conjuring a scent.” Later on, she was no longer associated with the very brand that carries her name. Alleche had parted ways with Lauder in 2006 and signed an exit agreement that barred her from the fragrance industry for five years. She soon realized she had made a mistake because “the hunger and spirit” never left her.

In 2011, Alleche launched a new brand that sells fragrances in store and online: Jo Loves. Although she was more than 20 years removed from mixing fragrances in her kitchen, she found herself back in a kitchen, doing what she loved — mixing and matching scents to capture the emotions she feels. Alleche re-trained herself. “I’d sit for hours with fragrance sticks — one in my left hand, one in my right — wafting them beneath my nose, seeing what the combinations would trigger.”

The secret to success and the secret to reinvention in the second chapter of one’s life is to stay true to the path that your heart puts you on. Alleche writes, “I may no longer own my old brand but I remained Alleche, the person; that’s who I am, a creator of fragrance…trust in the one thing you’re good at.” Alleche reminds us that all too often, we’re pulled in many directions. A friend gets a job in a hot industry and convinces you to follow his path, a colleague starts a company and wants you to join her, a boss invites you to fill a position that has a more prestigious title, but takes you away from what you enjoy. Alleche says in those situations, the best advice she can give is the one she’s learned from going from kitchen entrepreneur to millionaire: “Follow your heart. If you wake up each morning with a drive rooted in the passion of what you do, rather than a passion for the money you can make, you’re on a wise path.”
